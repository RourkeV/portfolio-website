User-Centered Design

Through this process, I developed stronger user research and empathy skills. I learned how to conduct interviews, create user personas, and design with accessibility in mind.

Interactions with local design studios and professionals gave me insight into the Spanish design industry and how cultural context influences design decisions and user expectations.

Classes on design systems and information architecture helped me understand how to create consistent, scalable design solutions that work across multiple platforms and devices.

Importantly, my academic experience was not limited to technical design skills. Classes on cultural aspects of design highlighted how visual communication is shaped by cultural contexts. This made me realize that effective design requires cultural awareness and the ability to adapt solutions for diverse audiences.

Overall, I would describe my academic experience as practice-based and iterative, combining design theory, user research, prototyping, and intercultural design thinking.

Intercultural Overview

Equally important to my academic growth has been the intercultural learning I experienced, both in the classroom and through day-to-day life in Catalonia, Spain.

Cultural Immersion

In formal settings, I participated in Catalan language classes and cultural workshops, where I learned about the region's unique history, traditions, and identity distinct from broader Spanish culture.

These experiences challenged me to think critically about regional identities and helped me appreciate the complexity of Spanish and Catalan culture.

Beyond the classroom, I immersed myself in local traditions. I experienced Catalan festivals, explored historic sites, and learned about the significance of local customs and celebrations.

Exploring different parts of Catalonia deepened my cultural learning. Visiting Barcelona, Tarragona, and the Pyrenees gave me a sense of Catalan history, landscapes, and architectural heritage. Walking through medieval towns, Roman ruins, and mountain trails helped me understand the region's rich cultural diversity.

Working in a multicultural team was another key intercultural experience. I learned to navigate different communication styles, design preferences, and work approaches. Our group activities (whether through exploring Lleida, enjoying tapas together, or collaborative design sessions) helped build stronger connections across cultures.

Looking back, I feel that my intercultural experience has given me greater cultural sensitivity and design thinking skills. I have become more adaptable, more aware of how culture influences design decisions, and more comfortable working in diverse international creative environments.
