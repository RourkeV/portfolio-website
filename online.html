Online Week

Virtual Introduction to the Global Minor Program

Before we all set off to travel around the world, the program began slowly with a series of online classes that introduced us to the expectations and intended outcomes of the minor. During this period, we were introduced not only to the other students participating in the program but also to the lecturers who would be guiding us throughout the experience.

Introduction to Subjects

We began the project gradually, starting the week with introductions to the five subjects that would form the foundation of the minor. The sessions were brief, providing only an overview of each subject, and I found it somewhat challenging to stay fully engaged during the online classes. However, the excitement started to bui;d as we came to understand what we where going to learn in the months ahead and how each subject would contribute to the development of our main project.

First Assignment & Project Group

For the first assignment, I was placed into a project group with fellow students. The purpose of this task was to allow us to become better acquainted, explore how we function as a team, and begin establishing a group dynamic. Although it was a relatively simple exercise, it helped me figure out how my grouped worked and what my role within the team was going to be for the next few months. Personally, I found it a challenge as I find that introductions and interactions over the internet make it difficult to get to know someone.

Introduction to Main Project

We were then introduced to the main project of the global minor, which forms the central focus of the program. During this introduction, I gained insight into the project's goals, expectations, and its relevance within a real-world context. This not only helped me to understand the broader impact of our teams but it also gave me a clearer sense of what is to come and how I could contribute to the overall outcome.

Academic Activities Overview

The academic activities during this initial phase included:

  • An introduction to the various subjects we would be studying over the three-month duration, providing a foundation for the work ahead.
  • A detailed explanation of the main project that would serve as the central focus of the minor, along with the introduction of our project teams.
  • A short, practical assignment designed to help each team get to know one another and begin developing group dynamics.

This gradual start allowed us to familiarize ourselves with the structure of the minor, connect with our team, and prepare for the challenges that lay ahead.
