Academic Overview

The ML/AI module at Hasselt was a deep dive into machine learning and neural networks, moving me beyond theory into confident application. Meeting our lecturers and classmates, I was struck by the diversity of perspectives and the welcoming atmosphere. Our studies focused on the fundamentals of machine learning and neural networks, and I found myself challenged to think critically and creatively.

Core Learning

The teaching style at the Belgian campus was both familiar and refreshingly different, prompting me to evaluate my own learning habits and adapt to new expectations. We tackled three assignments in week two, applying machine learning theory to practical problems. Working closely with classmates, I saw how diverse backgrounds and approaches can spark creative solutions.

Neural Networks

Gained hands-on experience building and training neural networks, understanding activation functions, backpropagation, and optimization techniques.

Practical Assignments

Completed three challenging assignments applying ML theory to real-world problems, developing both technical skills and problem-solving abilities.

Technical Assessment

Prepared for and completed a rigorous technical assessment from a company, refining skills and deepening understanding under pressure.

Team Collaboration

Worked with diverse teammates to integrate new concepts into our main project, learning the value of collaboration and clear communication.

Final Presentations

Presented our work to lecturers and peers, demonstrating mastery of ML/AI concepts and effective communication of technical ideas.

Teaching Style Comparison

Evaluated differences between Belgian and South African teaching approaches, adapting to new expectations and expanding learning strategies.

Looking back, this was a turning point in my academic journey. The ML/AI module gave me a real, working understanding of machine learning and neural networks. This period taught me the importance of perseverance, time management, and clear communication. I can see how much I grew, not just as a student, but as a collaborator and problem-solver.

Exploring Belgium

Beyond the classroom, Hasselt offered countless opportunities for cultural exploration and personal growth. My first days were a blend of excitement and challenge. Settling into new accommodation, I was immediately faced with the unexpected task of tracking down my lost laptop bag, a process that introduced me to the Belgian train system and the realities of navigating local bureaucracy. These early hurdles were more than logistical; they were my first lessons in resilience and adaptation.

Christmas Traditions

Exploring Hasselt and its festive Christmas markets offered a vivid contrast to celebrations back home, highlighting the unique rhythms of European life. The Christmas festival was a highlight, sharing glühwein, waffles, and laughter with friends and locals. These moments of celebration and relaxation were as important as any lecture, giving me space to reflect and recharge.

Arrival Challenges

Navigated the Belgian train system and bureaucracy while tracking down lost luggage, learning resilience and comparing systems to South Africa.

Christmas Festivals

Experienced traditional Belgian Christmas markets with glühwein, waffles, and ice skating, connecting with locals and building friendships.

Brussels Exploration

Visited the capital city to retrieve my laptop bag, exploring the royal palace, architecture, and history along the way.

Bruge Tour

Took a guided tour revealing the city's layered history and shifting fortunes, staying with a local student for an authentic experience.

Belgian Cuisine

Discovered the surprisingly good combination of mussels and chips, experiencing everyday Belgian life through food and hospitality.

Knokker Ocean Swim

Tested courage with a swim in the icy waters of Knokker, creating memorable stories and embracing discomfort for growth.

As my time in Hasselt came to an end, I traveled back to South Africa with a sense of accomplishment, gratitude, and excitement for what comes next. I realized that informal interactions often lead to the most meaningful cultural exchanges. These adventures reminded me that growth often comes from embracing discomfort and seeking out new experiences. Comparing the systems here to those in South Africa, I began to appreciate the subtle differences in how people approach everyday problems. The lessons from Hasselt will continue to shape how I learn, collaborate, and approach new opportunities in the future.
