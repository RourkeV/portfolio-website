Pretoria

Three weeks of sensor fixes, sunsets, and small adventures

Fieldwork & Fixes

Pretoria was our three-week sprint to finish strong. We finally got the sensors into Hartbeespoort Dam, watched what actually worked, and found all the messy bits we needed to tighten up.

Hartbeespoort Shake-down

Testing on the dam was equal parts sunrise views and scrambling to patch issues. Seeing live readings in the water made every late-night tweak worth it.

Week 1: Fix Frenzy

Logged the failures in the dam, rebuilt weak spots, and left with hardware and calibration finally behaving.

Week 2: Stakeholders

Mapped who cares about the data, drafted next steps, and lined up the handover plan after the project wraps.

Week 3: Final Pass

Polished the deck, rehearsed, and locked in the last open items before the final presentation.

Pretoria Walkabouts

Explored beyond the usual drive-through - new cafés, quick city detours, and finally seeing the place properly.

Weekends Away

Cape Town for ocean air and hikes; Margate for easy beach days on the wild coast.

Pilanesberg Surprise

Day trip detour turned epic - three leopard sightings in one run. Absolutely unreal.

By the end of the three weeks, the sensor stack was solid, the story made sense, and we had enough real-world proof to feel confident about the handoff.

Personal Reflection

Pretoria felt familiar yet new - I’d only ever passed through, never stayed. This time I actually lived in it: morning commutes to the dam, quick bites between fixes, and sunsets that made the long days feel lighter.

Small Team, Big Push

We got closer fast - debugging on the dam wall, swapping roles when someone hit a wall, and cheering when the data finally flowed.

Quick Escapes

Weekend sprints: Cape Town’s sea breeze and hikes, then Margate’s lazy swims. Each trip reset our brains before diving back in.

The Pilanesberg day trip is going to stick with me - three leopard sightings in one go felt like the universe giving us a nod after a chaotic week.

Leaving Pretoria meant saying goodbye to the team, the dam, and the rhythm we had built. We left tired, proud, and ready for whatever the next campus threw at us.
